DS200 Assignment: Analysis of R&D Expenditure

This project analyzes Research & Development (R&D) expenditure across various economic sectors in India for the fiscal years 2018–19 to 2020–21. The goal is to identify key trends, top-spending sectors, and the inequality in national R&D investment using Python libraries like pandas, matplotlib, and seaborn.

Data Source: R&D Expenditure by Economic Activity – data.gov.in

Visual Insights and Inferences

Scatter Plot: R&D Expenditure (2018–19 vs. 2019–20)

Observation: Most sectors lie close to the y = x line with a slight upward drift; one dominant outlier stands out.

Inference: Overall spending increased between years, with the Professional, Scientific and Technical Activities sector dominating investment.

Box Plot: Distribution of R&D Expenditure (2018–2021)

Observation: The data is highly right-skewed, showing a few sectors with very high spending compared to the rest.

Inference: R&D investment is heavily concentrated in select industries, revealing a significant inequality in national innovation funding across different sectors.

Bar Chart: Top 10 Sectors by R&D Expenditure (2020–21)

Observation: The Professional, Scientific and Technical Activities sector far outspends all others, followed by Agriculture and Human Health activities.

Inference: National priorities heavily favor scientific and technical R&D, with a notable and sustained emphasis on agriculture and healthcare.
