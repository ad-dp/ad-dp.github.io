Randomized M-SVD for Tensors

This project explores low-rank tensor approximations using a randomized M-Singular Value Decomposition (RM-SVD) algorithm. We analyze the trade-offs between computational cost and reconstruction error by varying transform matrices (M) and random sampling strategies (W). The method's effectiveness is demonstrated on a facial recognition task, comparing its accuracy and runtime against traditional SVD and other randomized variants.

Key Findings: An orthonormal transform (M) with a fully random sampling tensor (W) provided robust and accurate results for both tall and fat tensors, while an identity transform introduced bias in non-square cases.

Numerical Stability of ENSO Model

An analysis of the Recharge-Discharge Oscillator model for ENSO prediction, focusing on numerical stability and computational efficiency. This study compares the performance of different Strong Stability Preserving (SSP) Runge-Kutta methods and floating-point precisions (float16 vs. float64). We evaluated the impact of time step resolution on error accumulation and overall runtime.

Key Findings: Higher precision (float64) allows for the use of lower-order schemes without sacrificing accuracy, while lower precision (float16) is faster but more sensitive to round-off errors at smaller time steps.
